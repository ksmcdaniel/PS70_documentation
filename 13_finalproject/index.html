I will lay out a variety of healthy (baby carrots, peas, yuck, etc.) and unhealthy (reeses, Hersheyâ€™s kisses, Haribo gummy bears) snacks in different trays. The trays will have pressure sensors beneath them so that the dispenser can tell which snack I picked up. The robotic arm will need to be on a horizontal track so that it can move back and forth so it is ready to slap me if I choose unwisely. I started with the trickiest part of the project first: sliding the arm back and forth with a stepper motor. I had a lot of difficulty wiring the stepper motor correctly, but was glad that I started on this because it took a lot more time than expected. I then worked on the ultrasonic sensor components.

Stepper Motor

I noticed the stepper motor kept locking up and skipping steps, which would cause it to randomly start spinning the other direction. Part of this problem was fixed by increasing my power supply from 9V to 12V but I will need to keep iterating in the coming weeks. I will focus on making the slider move back and forth well.

Ultrasonic Sensor
