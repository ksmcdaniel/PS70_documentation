#1

Assignment 1: Make a Box.

Exploring kerf cuts and finger joints!

Project description

For this assignment, I wanted to deviate from the conventional box shape and add a distinctive curve on my box. By cutting slots partway through a material like wood or cardboard, the resistance to bending is greatly reduced. There were some great examples of kerf cut wood in the PS70 lab and I decided to experiment with cardboard. I used a rectangular pattern to make a sketch of my kerf cut in Fusion 360 and added it to the score layer in Rhino. This worked surprisingly well.

Closure & fit

My curved box lid had some elasticity to it and would not have stayed over the box if I didn't have some mechanism holding it in. I laser cut a little slot in the wall of my box that would accept a tab on the box lid. This worked well and allowed me to store the box in its closed position. I also experimented with the number of fingers on my box and found that with seven fingers there was enough friction to hold the box together without hot glue but the fingers were still big enough to not break.

CAD design

I already had some experience with Fusion360, but learned a variety of new functions throughout this process. The rectangular pattern was particularly useful for making evenly-spaced copies of the fingers and for extending my kerf cut pattern across the lid of the box. I also got more experience CADing with parameters.

Final Product

After cutting off all of the fingers of my box on my first attempt... I got a working final product! I am happy with how it turned out. No superglue needed!
