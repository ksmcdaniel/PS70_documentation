Project description

During freshman and sophomore year, whenever I felt like taking a cheat day, my roommate was always there to shame me about it. One time, he even slapped a HUDS brownie out of my handsâ€¦ Now heâ€™s taking a gap semester and I have nobody to stop me from testing the limits of human dessert consumption. So my plan is to replace my roommate with a robot.

I will lay out a variety of healthy (baby carrots, peas, yuck, etc.) and unhealthy (reeses, Hersheyâ€™s kisses, Haribo gummy bears) snacks in different trays. The trays will have pressure sensors beneath them so that the dispenser can tell which snack I picked up. There will be a robotic arm on the dispenser that can slap the unhealthy snacks out of my hand. If I pick up a healthy snack, the robotic arm wonâ€™t do anything. The robotic arm will need to be on a horizontal track so that it can move back and forth so it is ready to slap me if I choose unwisely. Depending on which snack I pick up, an LCD screen will display a mean or kind message.

I could also integrate a speaker/buzzer to play shameful sound effects when I pick up bad snacks. And LED strip could glow green for healthy and red for unhealthy.

I started with the trickiest part of the project first: sliding the arm back and forth with a stepper motor. I had a lot of difficulty wiring the stepper motor correctly, but was glad that I started on this because it took a lot more time than expected. I then worked on the ultrasonic sensor components.

Stepper Motor

I noticed the stepper motor kept locking up and skipping steps, which would cause it to randomly start spinning the other direction. Part of this problem was fixed by increasing my power supply from 9V to 12V but I will need to keep iterating in the coming weeks. I will focus on making the slider move back and forth well.

Ultrasonic Sensor

This part of the project went relatively smoothly. I was able to draw off of my work from electronic inputs week (week 6) to integrate the ultrasonic sensor. I will add three more sensors in the future to form a sensor array. Below is a picture of my oscilloscope reading from the trigger pin of my microcontroller.

Initial Setup

I decided to iterate the camera slider piece with the laser cutter intially, since it is way faster than 3D printing. I used an eccentric nut to tighten the wooden slider piece appropriately, which really helped. Here are pictures of my slider setup, the bins to hold snacks, and the ultrasonic sensor setup. It didn't look pretty but it got the job done.

In this initial test code, the stepper motor moves 80 steps in a set direction when the distance sensor reads more than 10 cm. The motor then stops.
In this test code, the stepper motor moves 80 steps in a set direction when the tray is lifted, stops for 1 second, then keeps moving in that direction. After the tray is returned, the stepper reverses and the camera slider returns to its initial position.

MVP

I added set screws into the aluminum extrusion holder pieces on each side and 3D printed a new slider piece that I could attach a servo to. I then revisited C++ class structure to make seperate classes for the servo motor, stepper motor, and ultrasonic sensor. Multi-tasking these three components took me a lot of time and I still need to work more on it in the coming weeks.
